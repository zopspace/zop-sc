About ZoP Stokes Croft

This is the first ZoP, our prototype. Places like Hamilton House, PRSC, Cafe Kino and the Cube Microplex are all Zones of Possibility and together they make Stokes Croft a great case study for regeneration from the ground-up. We hope this ZoP will inspire others to use culture, activism and risk-taking.

What is a Zone of Possibility?

A Zone of Possibility is a place where individuals can overcome the constraints of expectations and power structures to make things happen.



What is a ZoP?

A ZoP is a digital celebration of that achievement. It is a place where you can learn, discuss and prepare to create your own Zone of Possibility or ZoP.



How can I help?

We need feedback on the website and the wider project. You can help us by filling in the feedback form. This will help us make a good case to funders.



What’s the plan?

The plan is to get some funding to complete this ZoP. We’d like to fill it with content, do more work on our playlist generating back-end and learn how to facilitate people to make a ZoP. We’d like to develop this to proof of concept so that we can then help more people make new ZoPs.

Prof. John cook

John Cook (PhD) is Professor of Learning Innovation at University of the West of England. He is a member of the Centre for Moving Images Research where he leads the core Research Stream in Hybrid Reality and Culture.

Previous to this he was Professor of Technology Enhanced Learning (TEL) and Director at Learning Technology Research Institute, London Metropolitan University. John has over 14 years previous experience as a full-time lecturer at various Higher Education Institutions. He has over 16 years project management experience and has been part of research and development grant proposals that have attracted over £5 million in competitive external funding. FP7 examples include MATURE IP and Learning Layers IP. In addition, he has published/presented nearly 300 refereed articles and invited talks in the area of TEL, having a specific interest in several related areas: creative, working and learning city (diversity, entrepreneurship and culture in city regeneration/resilience), virtual and augmented reality, hybridity and hybrid cities, informal learning, mobile learning in all sectors, 3D web and visualisations, social web, and work-based learning. John is a founding member of The London Mobile Learning Group (www.londonmobilelearning.net/). He was Chair/President of the Association for Learning Technology (2004-06) and Chair of ALT’s Research Committee (2008-2012). John sits on various journal editorial boards and conducts Assessor and review work for the EU and UK Research Councils.

Dr Patricia Santos

Patricia Santos has got a Computer Engineering degree and a PhD in Information, Communication and Media Technologies, focused on the research areas of Technology Enhanced Learning (TEL) and Human Computer Interaction (HCI). In 2011 Patricia presented her PhD thesis (focused on new representation of test and assessment using interaction contexts such as: geo-located routes of questions answered with mobile phones, use of maps for representing questions and 3D virtual worlds for assessment environments) obtaining the qualification of Summa cum Laude. Patricia has been involved in several European and national R&D projects in the field of TEL. Her role has been especially focused in the design of Technology Enhanced Learning tools, development of pilots and experiments in learning/informal contexts, evaluation of research results, and organization of DBR workshops. In addition, Patricia has five years of teaching experience in different subjects (TEL, HCI, Programming…) in different universities (UPF, UAB and UOC) and public institutions (Cibernarium).

From 2007-2013, she was a researcher within the GTI research group (Interactive Technologies Group, UPF, Barcelona). Since 2013 (March) Patricia is researcher in Technology enhanced Learning in UWE working in the european research project Learning Layers: http://learning-layers.eu/ and researcher member of the ZoP.Space team.

Main Interests: Technology Enhanced Learning, Mobile Learning, Smart Cities, Human Computer Interaction, Design Based Research, Participatory Design.

Khawar Hasham

Khawar Hasham holds BSc IT (Hons.) in computer science from National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), Pakistan and an MPhil degree in computer science from the University of the West of England (UWE), UK. He has recently finished his PhD studies in computer science from UWE in December 2015. Khawar has research interests in the area of distributed computing, Grid and Cloud computing, virtualization and data management, workflow provenance and reproducibility. He has worked on workflow performance optimization to improve the execution performance of data workflows used at CMS CERN, Geneva. He has also contributed to various European Commission projects such as Health-e-Child, and neuGRID/N4U. He is a member of the Centre for Complex Cooperative Systems at UWE.

Main Interests: Cloud Computing, Grid Computing, Distributed Systems, Scientific Workflows, Provenance, Experiment Reproducibility, Software Development.
